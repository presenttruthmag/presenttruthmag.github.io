But having read some of what Arminius wrote, he would deny this appellation as not fairly representing his views.

It seems to me that the issue could be perhaps solved by clearly defining the meaning of the "meritorious" cause of salvation and clearly separating it from the "instrumental" cause or personal means of Man's salvation.

The "meritorious cause" being 1.) the humiliation and incarnation of our Lord, 2.) His sinless life lived as the Second Adam, 3.) His suffering for the sins of the world in our place, 4.) His atoning death on the cross to propitiate the wrath of God and make atonement for the justice of God in our place, 5.) and then the intercession of our Lord Jesus Christ in heaven mediating the merit of His blood for the benefit of His repentant believing saints on earth.

This salvation work of our Lord was promised to mankind and then accomplished outside of us and without any of our works since the beginning of the creation and is revealed to us through the proclamation of this "foolishness" as the apostle Paul describes the gospel of Jesus Christ. "In Christ" God has accomplished eternal salvation for the entire world.

The "instrumental cause" or personal means of salvation for fallen mankind on the other hand is being actively accomplished through the gracious work of the Holy Spirit upon the hearts of the disobedient children of Adam who are dead in trespass and sin and without hope before God. This work upon the human heart is not in any way meritorious, because it must always have the imputed merit of our Lord added to it for it to be acceptable in God's sight.

This instrumental cause is the work of the Holy Spirit in convicting all the world of sin and righteousness and judgment before the throne of God. Then He graciously "smites" us with the other side of His sword with the gracious proclamation that God Himself in the person of His Son the Lord Jesus Christ has come into the world, lived a sinless life, and died as an atoning death for the sins of all the world. Christ so loved the world that He has paid the sin debt for all the world.  

And not only that, He then offers the free gift of forgiveness and eternal life to all who will respond to His gracious offer of salvation. This offer of free salvation to all is attended by the outpouring of the Holy Spirit in "regenerating" or "preceding" or "prevenient" grace to all the world!! Luther was certainly enlightened about the work of the Holy Spirit upon the hearts of the lost human family.
