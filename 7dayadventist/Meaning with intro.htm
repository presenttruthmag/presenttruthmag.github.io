The principles of God's two great commandments to love the Lord with all our hearts and to love and treat our neighbors as we would want to be treated are to be lived out even when we find ourselves in a pagan society or under the rule of a godless government like the Roman empire indeed was. Our Lord was not teaching us that criminals and law breakers should not be prosecuted and punished by legitimate civil authorities in proportion to the seriousness of their crimes against society. Christ was expanding the far-reaching outward and inward claims of God's Law upon the believer's life. However, He was not eliminating the need for, and pursuit of, legitimate social justice and equity for the individual person and society as a whole.

Unbiblical Teaching #11. The belief that the ritually clean / unclean Levitical food laws are binding upon Christians.

Correction: (The Levitical ritual food laws were never mandated and imposed upon the Christian believers by the apostles as a test of Christian fellowship in the early church of the first century. Christ cleansed all foods of their ritual uncleanness (Mark 7:19), but those who wish to abstain for health reasons should not mandate this for others who do not choose to abstain.)

Unbiblical Distinctive #12. The belief by some that just before Christ comes again and the Lord leaves the most holy place of the heavenly sanctuary, the intercession of Christ's imputed merit will no longer be necessary for the Christian remnant, because they supposedly have become "perfected" sinless believers and are as sinless as Jesus and as sinless as Adam before the Fall.

Correction: In the same way as Noah and his family were shut in the ark after the door of salvation for the wicked antediluvian world was closed, the intercession of Christ's imputed righteousness never ceases for Christ's remnant people. The reason that Christ never stops His intercession and imputed righteousness for the repentant believer in Him, is because the sanctified life of the commandment-keeping believer will still need the imputed righteousness of Christ to cover him even until the Lord comes in the clouds of heaven.The merit of His blood continues for the repentant believing remnant who are keeping the commandments of God and trusting in Christ's blood until He comes again. However, probation does in fact close for the unrepentant unbelieving world. But probation closes only for the unbelieving lawless world which rejects the Lord's final proclamation of the three angel's messages of Revelation 14. They reject the invitation to repent of their transgressions of God's holy Law, including the fourth commandment. They refuse to call upon Him as their Savior and Lord.

Unbiblical Teaching #13. The belief that there is a "blotting out of sin" as a special or "final atonement" for the people of God which is different than the daily forgiveness of sins.

Correction: The term "blotting out of sin" and the term "forgiveness of sin " are used interchangeably in the Bible.

Colossians 2:13-14 (KJV) And you, being dead in your sins and the uncircumcision of your flesh, hath he quickened together with him, having forgiven you all trespasses; Blotting out (exaleipho) the handwriting of ordinances that was against us, which was contrary to us, and took it out of the way, nailing it to his cross;

Colossians 2:13-14 (NAU) And you, who were dead in your trespasses and the uncircumcision of your flesh, God made alive together with him, having forgiven us all our trespasses,by canceling (exaleipho) the record of debt that stood against us with its legal demands. This he set aside, nailing it to the cross.

Colossians 2:13-14 (NLT) You were dead because of your sins and because your sinful nature was not yet cut away. Then God made you alive with Christ. He forgave all our sins. He canceled (exaleipho) the record that contained the charges against us. He took it and destroyed it by nailing it to Christ's cross.

Colossians 2:13-14 (NRS) And when you were dead in trespasses and the uncircumcision of your flesh, God made you alive together with him, when he forgave us all our trespasses, erasing (exaleipho) the record that stood against us with its legal demands. He set this aside, nailing it to the cross.

Acts 3:18-21 (KJV) But what God foretold by the mouth of all the prophets, that his Christ would suffer, he thus fulfilled. Repent therefore, and turn again, that your sins may be blotted out (exaleipho), that times of refreshing may come from the presence of the Lord, and that he may send the Christ appointed for you, Jesus, whom heaven must receive until the time for restoring all the things about which God spoke by the mouth of his holy prophets long ago.

Isaiah 44:22 (KJV) I have blotted out, as a thick cloud, thy transgressions, and, as a cloud, thy sins: return unto me; for I have redeemed thee.

Unbiblical Teaching #14. The belief that the "latter rain" is a special final second blessing and infilling of the Holy Spirit which totally restores the character of God's people to the original innocence of Adam before the Fall.

Correction: (All repentant baptized forgiven believers in Christ receive the gift of the Holy Spirit in both "former and latter rain" measure and begin the daily life-long battle with sin and begin to daily grow in holiness and sanctification until the Last Day at our Lord's second coming. Repentant believers in Christ will need the forgiveness of sin until the end of time. The sanctification and imparted righteousness of the most mature believers in Christ must still be covered by the imputed righteousness of Christ. Prior to the fall of Adam, he did not need the imputed righteousness of Christ. He had neither a sinful nature nor any propensity to sin.)

Unbiblical Teaching #15. The belief in sinless perfection for the "final generation" before Christ comes.

Correction: (While all true Christians are seeking to walk in holiness, obedience to the holy Law of God, and a sanctified life, total sinless perfection for the Christian community comes at translation. Repentance and forgiveness are the "daily bread" of the believing Christian until "this corruptible shall put on incorruption" at the coming of Christ on the Last Day. God's people will need both justification, the daily forgiveness of sin, and on-going growth in sanctification until the Lord comes in the clouds of heaven.)

Unbiblical Teaching #16. The belief that the "last days", the "time of the end" began in A.D.1798.

Correction: (The "last lays" and the "time of the end" began with the gospel proclamation in the apostolic era.)

Unbiblical Teaching # 17. The belief that just prior to the coming of the Lord there will be a "special" group of Christians called the "144,000" who keep the Law of God perfectly and reach sinless perfection prior to the second coming of our Lord .

Correction: (John's epistle of Revelation 7 and 14 figuratively depicts spiritual Israel in symbolic language as the "twelve tribes of Israel" and calls the Christian church the "144,000". This symbolism represents all true believers in Christ from Adam unto the final Day at the coming of Christ. All repentant sinners who trust in the merits of the Second Adam, Jesus Christ, are members of spiritual Israel, the "144,000". There are no special groups in the kingdom of God. Jesus Christ alone has provided atonement for sin and He alone is the sinless One. All repentant sinners who trust in His sinless life and atoning death are justified by God's unmerited grace through faith in His propitiation of sin at Calvary and have His sinless life and atoning death imputed and reckoned to them. The symbols of the "144,000" and the "innumerable multitude" represent the same identical group, spiritual "Israel".)

Conclusion: These reforms in Seventh-day Adventist teaching are absolutely necessary in order for a true reformation and revival of primitive godliness to take place among the advent people.

Acts 17:30 "The times of ignorance God overlooked, but now he commands all people everywhere to repent,"

Then and only then will the people of God in other folds begin to take the Seventh-day Adventist gospel proclamation seriously and join this God-ordained movement to restore the bible and "only the bible" as the rule of Christian faith.

Seventh-day Adventism is not a cult. The essence of the evangelical Adventist movement is true historic Protestant Christianity. Seventh-day Adventism, as God intends it to be, is God's final warning to all the world to repent and be justified by Christ's unmerited grace alone through faith in the sinless life and atoning death of Christ alone as revealed in the Bible alone.

This is the true Seventh-day Adventism which the Lord Jesus Christ intended for this movement to be. Christ's purpose for providentially raising up this end-time movement was for Seventth-day Adventists to finish the Protestant reformation and be the foremost voice in all the world to proclaim the commandments of God and uplift the sinless, crucified, and risen Savior as the atoning sacrifice for the sins of all the world. It is time for the Seventh-day Adventist movement to finish the Protestant reformation and become all that the Lord intended this movement to be.
