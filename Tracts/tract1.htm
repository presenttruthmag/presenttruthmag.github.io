YOUR SINS ARE FORGIVEN!!

Yes! That’s absolutely right! God has forgiven your sins! If ever there was one thing that was absolutely certain, absolutely true, it is that all your sins are forgiven. Where’s the proof that your sins are forgiven? How can we know it? The proof that your sins are forgiven was openly made certain and is promised to all the world because the Lord Jesus Christ, our Creator and Redeemer, has come to this Earth, lived an absolutely sinless spotless life, and then He died upon the Cross in your place for your sins so that you may be forgiven and be accepted and counted as if you had never committed one single sin in your whole life.
Because Jesus was condemned for our sins, all of the human family has been acquitted of guilt before the throne of God. You have been found innocent at the judgment bar of God. In Christ there is no condemnation, no guilt, no sin, no accusation, no death, no more nagging conscience. In Jesus Christ there is true freedom! This freedom also includes freedom from fear of death. Jesus Christ has arisen from the grave and now He lives! And not only that; He promises that anyone who believes in His death for their sins will one day be raised from the dead and live eternally with Him forever. That’s right!! Those who put their faith in Jesus Christ have eternal life!! Isn’t this Good News? It really is, isn’t it? God loves you and all of us so much that He came to Earth to die for our sins so that we might be forgiven and that we might have the promise of eternal life!

Will you believe in Jesus Christ as your Saviour from sin and as Lord of your life? What does it mean to have Jesus as the Lord of your life? It’s very simple! Just as Jesus has loved you and forgiven you, he asks you to love your fellow man and forgive everyone else where they have sinned against you. God has forgiven you! Will you accept your forgiveness and forgive others? God has loved you!! Will you love others as He has loved you?

Let this short prayer be your prayer: "Lord Jesus, thank you for coming into this world to die upon the cross for my sins. Please forgive me for all of my sins and please begin to live in my heart as my Savior and Lord. Thank you for your promise of forgiveness and eternal life. In Jesus' name I pray. Amen."

Now that you have heard and believed the Good News about Jesus, why not tell everyone you know about His forgiveness and His love.
